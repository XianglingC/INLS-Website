About Me

“I’m not constantly running away from humans, but I believe that by living on volcanoes, away from humans, I’ll end up loving humans.” This quote from Fire of Love encapsulates my relationship with the environment and best describes my passion for Environmental Science and Information Science.

Nature—especially forests—has always been my refuge from the noisy, fast-paced human world, a place where I can find peace. In nature, unlike the complex and often overwhelming interactions among people, everything feels pure and direct. My senses become heightened: I feel the rough texture of tree bark, touch the soil with my hands, hear the gentle flow of a stream, and breathe in clean, fresh air. These experiences inspired me to seek volunteer opportunities in natural environments. During my time as a trail monitor in North Carolina’s forests and at the NC Botanical Garden last year, I discovered that the joy I found in nature extended beyond myself and benefited others as well. Whether by preventing injuries from falling branches or creating safe, peaceful spaces for visitors to rest and appreciate their surroundings, my work demonstrated how environmental stewardship can positively impact human well-being. Through these experiences, I realized that working with the environment is also a way of giving back to society, which led me to pursue Environmental Science as a field of study.

I see myself as a problem solver—someone who connects personal interests with real-world impact. To me, problem-solving involves understanding an issue, defining it clearly, and developing effective solutions. This mindset reflects my long-term academic and professional goals: to continue learning, address real-world challenges, and create value for both society and myself. This is where Information Science and GIS become essential. These quantitative tools allow me to organize, visualize, and analyze data in structured ways, helping me better understand environmental and energy-related problems. By combining data-driven analysis with environmental knowledge, I aim to develop informed and practical solutions.

Ultimately, my story began with an attempt to escape from humanity, but I have come to realize that I deeply care about it. Humans have taught me how to love and how to care—some of the most meaningful experiences of my life. Applying knowledge from Environmental Science, Information Science, and GIS to address global challenges and contribute to a better future is my way of giving back while staying true to my personal values. As another quote from Fire of Love states, “Volcanoes are the most beautiful things in nature, but they kill. And one of my dreams is that volcanoes no longer kill.” This sentiment perfectly captures both my professional ambitions and my desire to harmonize the beauty of nature with the well-being of humanity.
